### Career #1: Software Engineer/Developer (Example/Reference)

#### What They Do
Describe the main responsibilities and daily tasks:
Software Engineers design, develop, test, and maintain software systems. They are responsible for the entire life cycle of an application, from initial concept to deployment and updates. Daily tasks involve writing and reviewing code, debugging, planning new features, collaborating with cross-functional teams (product managers, designers), and ensuring software scalability and security. They often specialize in a specific domain (e.g., mobile apps, operating systems, cloud services).

#### Required Education & Skills
- **Minimum Education:** Bachelor's Degree in Computer Science or a related field.
- **Recommended Education:** Master's Degree for specialized or leadership roles (e.g., Principal Engineer).
- **Key Technical Skills:** Strong proficiency in one or more major programming languages (e.g., Python, Java, C++, JavaScript), data structures and algorithms, version control (Git), and software development methodologies (Agile/Scrum).
- **Important Soft Skills:** Critical thinking, complex problem-solving, collaboration, continuous learning, and communication (explaining technical concepts to non-technical stakeholders).

#### Salary Information
- **Entry-Level Salary Range:** $75,000 - $110,000
- **Mid-Career Salary Range:** $110,000 - $150,000
- **Senior-Level Salary Range:** $150,000 - $200,000+ (significantly higher in major tech hubs)

#### Job Outlook
- **Growth Rate (next 10 years):** 25% (Much faster than average)
- **Demand Level:** Very High. This career is the backbone of the digital economy.

### Career #2: Web Developer (Front-End/Back-End/Full-Stack)

#### What They Do
Describe the main responsibilities and daily tasks:
Web Developers design, build, and maintain websites and web applications. **Front-End** developers focus on the client-side (user interface and experience) using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. **Back-End** developers focus on server-side logic, APIs, and database integration. **Full-Stack** developers handle both. Daily tasks include coding new features, debugging across different browsers/devices, optimizing site speed, and collaborating with design and marketing teams.

#### Required Education & Skills
- **Minimum Education:** Associate's Degree or a recognized Coding Bootcamp/Certificate.
- **Recommended Education:** Bachelor's Degree in Computer Science, IT, or a related field with a strong portfolio of projects.
- **Key Technical Skills:** HTML, CSS, JavaScript (essential), proficiency in a framework (e.g., React, Angular, Node.js), responsive design principles, and version control (Git). Back-end also requires knowledge of a server-side language (Python, PHP, Java) and SQL.
- **Important Soft Skills:** Creativity, attention to detail (minor code errors can break layouts), collaboration, and rapid adaptability to new tools and frameworks.

#### Salary Information
- **Entry-Level Salary Range:** $58,000 - $78,000
- **Mid-Career Salary Range:** $77,000 - $95,000
- **Senior-Level Salary Range:** $98,000 - $140,000+

#### Job Outlook
- **Growth Rate (next 10 years):** $\sim 16\%$ (Much faster than average)
- **Demand Level:** High. Driven by the massive growth in e-commerce and mobile-optimized web services.

#### Connection to Your Coursework
Which of your current classes/skills connect to this career?
- [X] Programming
- [X] SQL/Databases
- [X] Web Design
- [ ] AI/ML

**How they connect:**
The **Web Design** module (HTML/CSS) is the direct foundation. **Programming** skills are essential for all JavaScript-based interactivity and any back-end logic. **SQL/Databases** skills are needed for full-stack and back-end development to store and manage site data.

#### Interesting Facts or Notes
What surprised you or seemed interesting about this career?
A Web Developer's portfolio of completed, live projects is often more critical to hiring than a traditional degree, demonstrating that practical skills and creativity are highly valued in this field.

### Career #3: Data Scientist

#### What They Do
Describe the main responsibilities and daily tasks:
Data Scientists use analytical, statistical, and programming skills to collect, clean, and interpret complex data sets. They build **predictive models** and **machine learning algorithms** to uncover trends and insights that inform strategic business decisions. Day-to-day work often involves data cleaning/wrangling (which can take up to 80% of their time), running statistical analysis, developing and validating models, and creating visualizations and reports to present their findings to non-technical stakeholders.

#### Required Education & Skills
- **Minimum Education:** Bachelor's Degree in Computer Science, Statistics, Mathematics, or a related quantitative field.
- **Recommended Education:** Master's Degree is often preferred or required for advanced roles, as it provides deep specialization in statistics and machine learning.
- **Key Technical Skills:** Proficiency in programming (primarily **Python** and **R**), **SQL** for data retrieval, strong statistical and mathematical modeling expertise, and experience with machine learning frameworks (e.g., scikit-learn, TensorFlow).
- **Important Soft Skills:** Analytical thinking, critical thinking, intellectual curiosity (asking the right questions of the data), and excellent communication for translating complex data results into simple, actionable business recommendations.

#### Salary Information
- **Entry-Level Salary Range:** $85,000 - $110,000
- **Mid-Career Salary Range:** $110,000 - $145,000
- **Senior-Level Salary Range:** $145,000 - $180,000+ (High demand areas can exceed $200k+)

#### Job Outlook
- **Growth Rate (next 10 years):** 34\% (Much faster than average)
- **Demand Level:** Very High. The role is driven by the massive, growing need for companies across all industries to leverage big data and AI for competitive advantage.

#### Connection to Your Coursework
Which of your current classes/skills connect to this career?
- [X] Programming
- [X] SQL/Databases
- [ ] Web Design
- [X] AI/ML

**How they connect:**
**Programming** (Python is a core language) and **SQL/Databases** are essential for data manipulation and retrieval. The **AI/ML** module is the direct foundation, covering the machine learning models and statistical techniques that form the core of a Data Scientist's work.

#### Interesting Facts or Notes
What surprised you or seemed interesting about this career?
Despite the focus on advanced modeling, Data Scientists spend the majority of their time on the least glamorous part of the job: **cleaning and preparing** data, which is often messy and incomplete in the real world.
